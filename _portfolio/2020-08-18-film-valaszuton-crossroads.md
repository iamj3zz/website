- type: text
  title: The Project
  content: |
    The film emerged from the **Self-Awareness Film Workshop**, a collaborative project between filmmaker Chilton and young people in crisis — those living in temporary homes, foster care, and at-risk situations. Rather than speaking *about* these communities from outside, the film centers their lived experiences, their language, their world. This is cinema created *with* the vulnerable, not *for* them — a crucial distinction that gives the work its raw authenticity and moral weight.

    The result is a message film that works at multiple levels: as social commentary on child trafficking and human exploitation, as an artistic statement about filmmaking in solidarity with marginalized youth, and as a provocative call to awareness in audiences most removed from these realities.
- type: text
  title: The Soundtrack
  content: |
    J3ZZ composed and produced the original soundtrack — an immersive sonic landscape that underscores the film's gritty realism without sentiment. The music serves the story's moral complexity: it neither condemns nor excuses, but witnesses. By refusing easy emotional resolution, the soundtrack deepens the film's capacity to provoke reflection.

    J3ZZ chose to participate in this project because of the film's explicit mission to raise awareness about sex trafficking and exploitation among orphaned and foster youth — a population at disproportionate risk. Beyond festival screenings, **VÁLASZÚTON / CROSSROADS** is shown in schools, used in educational contexts to initiate difficult but necessary conversations with young people about vulnerability, predation, and systemic failures.

    By composing for this film, J3ZZ contributed to a work designed not primarily as art for art's sake, but as art in service of protection and consciousness — a use of music aligned with human dignity and social responsibility.
