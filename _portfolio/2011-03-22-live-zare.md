  Violinist for Zene a Rasszizmus Ellen (ZARE), Hungary's adaptation of the international Music Against Racism movement, performing at Anti-Racism World Day events and the Daniel Pearl World Music Days.
description: |
  ZARE builds on the legacy of the British Rock Against Racism (1970s) and Love Music Hate Racism (LMHR) movements. Founded in 2009 with support from the Budapest British Embassy and coordinated by the Szubjektív Értékek Alapítvány (Subjective Values Foundation), ZARE channels the transformative power of music to promote social unity and challenge discrimination. The movement operates on the principle that "music has the power to unite, to give strength, and to effect positive social change." Rather than a political statement, it testifies to human equality through shared cultural experience.

  J3ZZ performed at recurring Anti-Racism World Day (Antirasszista Világnap) events at Budapest's Millenáris Park cultural complex, and at the Daniel Pearl World Music Days concert at Bazaar Klub — an international music network celebrating tolerance and cultural diversity through live performance.
