description: |
  J3ZZ has been an electronic and violinist improviser musician with Willany Léo since 2009, collaborating in real-time collective creation through movement and sound. Founded in 2008 by choreographer and dance pedagogue Zoltán Grecsó, Willany Léo operates as an improvisational dance theatre and creative community workshop where no pre-learned choreography exists—instead, dancers and musicians make instantaneous artistic decisions during each performance, creating genuine risk and unpredictability.

  ## The Concept: Open Score Methodology

  Willany Léo's distinguishing approach centers on the "Open Score"—a methodology balancing freedom with structure. As the ensemble articulates it: "The freedom of presence and the constraint of structure are not opposites, but a dynamic that strengthens each other." Rather than repeating the same choreography nightly, performances employ varying approaches—abstract or narrative compositions, pre-established "rules of the game" that guide improvisation, or completely open processes where everything emerges organically. The work prioritizes conscious embodiment, enabling consciousness to emerge from every part of the body.

  ## Collaboration & Knowledge Transmission

  J3ZZ's participation centers on supporting the ensemble's commitment to sharing and transmitting knowledge and experience across all generations of dance movement performers and musicians. The focus is on collective improvisation practice—creating spaces where diverse performers collaborate in real-time, building genuine connection between artists and audiences through participatory experiences, workshops, and introductory programs that demystify contemporary improvisation.

  ## Recognition & Context

  The ensemble participated in the EU-funded Beyond Fronta: Bridging New Territories project (2012–2014), earning a Success Story designation, and received the László Lábán Special Award in May 2019. Weekly performances take place at MU Színház's Káva Room (Wednesday, 20:30–21:30 local time). Regular involvement includes improvisation-based movement/sound experimentations performed for audiences across Hungary, particularly in Budapest, spanning galleries, theatres, and festivals.
