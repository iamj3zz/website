  - type: text
    title: "The Moment"
    content: |
      Three people. One room. No plot twists, no dramatic revelations, no climactic confrontation. Yet everything changes.

      Director Declan Hannigan's precise eye captures what remains unspoken — the weight of a glance, the hesitation before raising a glass, the knowledge that passes silently between bodies. The film is an exercise in restraint and observation, trusting the audience to read the emotional temperature of the space and understand that sometimes the most profound truths require no dialogue.

      This is cinema built on subtlety: the power of what is *not* said, the intimacy of shared knowledge, the fragility of the bonds we assume are solid.

  - type: text
    title: "The Music"
    content: |
      J3ZZ's score for **Best Friend's Wife's Lover** navigates the emotional precision of the narrative without sentimentality. The music respects the silence, punctuating the moment with careful restraint — present enough to deepen the emotional weight, invisible enough to preserve the intimacy of the room.

      The composer's role is to be a witness, not a guide. The music becomes part of the unspoken conversation, reflecting the undercurrents and tensions that the three characters carry silently with them.
