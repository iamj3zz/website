abstract: |
  Interdisciplinary improvisation coaching for young artists across Europe, as part of FOLLOW-UP — a Leonardo da Vinci EU-funded project by Roots & Routes International Association exploring non-formal learning in music, dance, and audiovisual arts.
description: |
  FOLLOW-UP was a Leonardo da Vinci programme project (2009–2011) by Roots & Routes International Association, building on the Roots & Routes Summercourses (2006–2008). The project brought together young artists from across Europe to develop competencies in music, dance, and audiovisual media through non-formal learning, peer coaching, and digital portfolios.

  J3ZZ participated as a coach in two sessions. In Budapest (February 2011), hosted by Subjective Values Foundation at their Ráday utca venue, he coached a multicultural group of 6 young adult artists across media, dance, and music — guiding them through the production and staging of their own performance. In Barcelona (May 2–14, 2011), hosted by Fundació Bosch i Gimpera, he coached a group of 10 young adult artists in the same interdisciplinary format.

  Both sessions centred on sharing performance experience and supporting participants in realising their own artistic projects — from conception through to stage production. The Budapest group performed at Volt Festival as part of the project's public programme.
