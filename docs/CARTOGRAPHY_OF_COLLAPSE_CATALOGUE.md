## Foreword

There are maps that describe the world as it is. And there are maps that describe the world as it breaks.

*Cartography of Collapse* belongs to the second kind.

These sixteen drawings do not show roads, borders, or coastlines. They trace something older and more fundamental: the geological forces that shape, fracture, compress, and ultimately dissolve matter itself. Each work is a stratum — a frozen moment in a process that unfolds across millions of years, rendered in a single, unrepeatable gesture of ink.

The series moves in two acts. The first, *Igneous*, begins in density — primordial matter at its most compact, its most mineral, its most solid. The second, *Erosion*, follows what happens after: the slow, inevitable dissolution of all solid things into void.

Every line in these drawings was made in one take, without revision. The improvisation is the work. Each drawing is a direct imprint of that instant — a record of pressure, release, and the forces in between.

These are not illustrations of collapse. They are collapse, arrested.

---

## Artist Statement

I have always been fascinated by deep time — the idea that the ground beneath us is not stable, not permanent, but a momentary configuration of forces that have been reshaping the earth's surface for billions of years.

*Cartography of Collapse* emerged from that fascination. I wanted to make the invisible visible: the geological strata beneath our feet, the tectonic pressures building toward rupture, the erosion that hollows mountains into archipelagos of sand.

Each drawing is improvised — one take, no corrections. I work with ink because it is irreversible. The mark is made and cannot be unmade. In this sense, the process mirrors what I am drawing: geological events are also irreversible. A canyon carved by a river over ten thousand years cannot be uncarved.

The sixteen works form a single narrative: from the density of igneous rock to the dispersal of eroded matter. But they can also be read individually — each one a world unto itself, a particular configuration of line and void that has never existed before and will never exist again.

## I. IGNEOUS

*Dense, primordial, mineral — matter in its most compact form*

The eight works of *Igneous* begin in darkness and density. These are drawings of formation: of geological pressure, of matter compressed into shape, of the moment before rupture. The palette is black on white, void cutting through mineral mass.

---

### 1 — Fractured System

![Fractured System](WORKS/PRINT/300p_B1.png)

**Ink on paper — Portrait — 29.7 × 42 × 0.1 cm — 2026 — Original, non-editioned**

An elegant curved arc emerges from absolute darkness — a single unified form defined by flowing topographic lines. The arc suggests movement suspended in time: water flowing, land folding, matter bending under immense pressure yet remaining graceful and coherent.

The arc is complete and whole, yet carved entirely from the surrounding void. This paradox — solidity defined by emptiness, form emerging from pure negation — is the essence of geological presence itself.

*Fractured System* captures the moment when primordial matter achieves its most elegant form: the instant where pressure and release create beauty, where geological forces compress into a single, memorable curve.

---

### 2 — Void Emergence

![Void Emergence](WORKS/PRINT/300p_B2.png)

**Ink on paper — Landscape — 42 × 29.7 × 0.1 cm — 2026 — Original, non-editioned**

Where *Fractured System* showed formation, *Void Emergence* reveals what lies beneath: immense emptiness. Deep gorges carve through mineral deposits, their walls rendered in white lines against the surrounding blackness. The voids are so substantial they dominate the composition — absence becomes presence.

These are canyons at their deepest, where erosion has hollowed the earth into vast chambers. Geological time measured in the slow, inexorable work of wind and water.

The voids are not negative space but active forces — the inevitable result of matter encountering time.

---

### 3 — Debris Field

![Debris Field](WORKS/PRINT/300p_B3.png)

**Ink on paper — Portrait — 29.7 × 42 × 0.1 cm — 2026 — Original, non-editioned**

Matter at maximum fragmentation, yet still holding together. Dense topographic lines create an almost biological texture — winding rivers, fractured strata, organic deposition. A black serpentine form cuts through the composition like a vein, suggesting flow and movement through a rigidified landscape.

Every inch pulses with geological activity: erosion channels, sediment deposits, the accumulated evidence of forces moving through matter. Chaotic yet coherent — all complexity emerging from a single underlying logic.

*Debris Field* is the moment of maximum disorder: chaos still coherent, before everything finally lets go.

---

### 4 — Layered Collapse

![Layered Collapse](WORKS/PRINT/300p_B4.png)

**Ink on paper — Portrait — 29.7 × 42 × 0.1 cm — 2026 — Original, non-editioned**

A definitive horizontal line splits this topography into two incompatible realities. Above: orderly parallel lines suggesting regular structure, an organized sky, some remnant of stability. Below: dark chaotic mass, collapse, matter returning to formlessness.

This is the threshold moment — the instant the system fails. Everything above the line is what was; everything below is what remains. The strata literally peel apart, revealing that all the order was only surface, and that beneath lies chaos waiting to reclaim the world.

A single line holding two incompatible truths.

---

### 5 — Scattered Archipelago

![Scattered Archipelago](WORKS/PRINT/300p_B5.png)

**Ink on paper — Landscape — 42 × 29.7 × 0.1 cm — 2026 — Original, non-editioned**

After systemic collapse, unity dissolves into archipelago. Two substantial masses of mineral material float separately in an ocean of absolute black — each complete in itself, yet utterly disconnected from the other. The void between them is vast and unbridgeable.

Each mass retains complex topography, evidence of its own geological history, yet they no longer communicate. What was once a single system has fragmented into isolated islands of matter, each surrounded by the void that now defines their relationship.

*Scattered Archipelago* is permanent fragmentation — two masses once one, now entirely isolated, yet each still complete.

---

### 6 — Saturated Network

![Saturated Network](WORKS/PRINT/300p_B6.png)

**Ink on paper — Portrait — 29.7 × 42 × 0.1 cm — 2026 — Original, non-editioned**

Vertical striations dominate this topography — as if the entire landscape has been compressed and stretched, its structure aligned by immense pressure. Every inch of the page is saturated with parallel lines, creating an almost textile-like surface. There is almost no void, no breathing room; the entire space is filled with structured density.

Matter under maximum stress — compressed yet maintaining rigid geometric order. The vertical lines suggest force pushing matter downward into increasing density.

*Saturated Network* is maximum compression: where pressure becomes not just force but presence, where structure and collapse nearly converge.

---

### 7 — Fossilized Echo

![Fossilized Echo](WORKS/PRINT/300p_B7.png)

**Ink on paper — Landscape — 42 × 29.7 × 0.1 cm — 2026 — Original, non-editioned**

Every part of the composition seethes with indecipherable texture — the cumulative effect of all geological forces working simultaneously, forms so tightly interwoven that no individual element remains readable.

Yet beneath this chaos lies order: strata of deep time, the compressed record of billions of years of transformation. What appears as noise is the signature of infinite complexity.

*Fossilized Echo* is the culmination of the igneous phase — matter so transformed by geological forces that only echoes of its original forms remain. Maximum chaos containing a perfect record.

---

### 8 — Compressed Time

![Compressed Time](WORKS/PRINT/300p_B8.png)

**Ink on paper — Landscape — 42 × 29.7 × 0.1 cm — 2026 — Original, non-editioned**

Radiating lines fan outward like an explosion frozen in stone — as if the matter itself is being compressed toward a central point then released outward simultaneously. Geological forces collide and transform, creating dynamic radiating patterns that suggest deep time compressed into a single instant.

This topography captures the moment when geological forces exceed the resistance of solid rock. The radiating pattern follows the logic of strain and release, force and counter-force.

*Compressed Time* is geology's paradox: epochs compressed into moments of transformation, vast forces rendered as fine lines. The instant tectonic forces collide into explosive change.

## II. EROSION

*Dispersed, fragmented, scattered — matter in dissolution*

The eight works of *Erosion* begin where *Igneous* ends — at the moment of rupture — and follow matter as it disperses. The palette inverts: white grounds, dark fragments floating in increasing void. These are drawings of aftermath, of dissolution, of the slow return of form to emptiness.

---

### 9 — Last Coordinate

![Last Coordinate](WORKS/PRINT/300p_W1.png)

**Ink on paper — Landscape — 42 × 29.7 × 0.1 cm — 2026 — Original, non-editioned**

A winding channel cuts through the composition — neither purely geometric nor purely organic, but a threshold between order and chaos. Above, dense topographic texture seethes with complexity; below, parallel lines suggest organized structure. The channel itself marks the boundary where one logic gives way to another.

This is the boundary between mapping and unmapping. On one side the world still makes sense; on the other, complexity begins to resist description.

*Last Coordinate* captures the moment of final demarcation — where measurement and order can still hold, before the territory refuses all language.

---

### 10 — Grid Zero

![Grid Zero](WORKS/PRINT/300p_W2.png)

**Ink on paper — Portrait — 29.7 × 42 × 0.1 cm — 2026 — Original, non-editioned**

From above, the landscape reveals itself as fragmented islands of mineral material connected by eroded channels. This aerial perspective shows the true topology of collapse: what appeared solid from ground level is revealed as already dispersed when viewed from distance.

Diagonal lines suggest flow — the movement of water, wind, and matter across the eroding landscape. Some areas show organized linearity; others dissolve into scattered fragments.

*Grid Zero*: solid ground was never solid at all, only appearing so from below.

---

### 11 — Horizon Dissolution

![Horizon Dissolution](WORKS/PRINT/300p_W3.png)

**Ink on paper — Landscape — 42 × 29.7 × 0.1 cm — 2026 — Original, non-editioned**

The horizon vanishes into white void. What should be a line separating earth from sky dissolves into uniform emptiness. Around the edges of the composition, fragments of topography remain — islands of form surrounded by dissolution.

The composition is profoundly disorienting: no clear orientation, no stable ground, no sky. All spatial references have dissolved into the white void at the center.

*Horizon Dissolution* is the moment when spatial orientation becomes impossible — the horizon's disappearance is the loss of the final reference point telling us which way is up.

---

### 12 — Inverted Territory

![Inverted Territory](WORKS/PRINT/300p_W4.png)

**Ink on paper — Portrait — 29.7 × 42 × 0.1 cm — 2026 — Original, non-editioned**

The relationship between matter and void inverts. What was dense becomes sparse; what was solid becomes scattered. Islands of dark topographic material float in white void, each isolated and complete in itself, yet fundamentally disconnected from all others.

A concentric ripple in the center suggests a single moment of disturbance — a pebble dropped in the void. Yet the islands remain unmoved: fragmentation, once complete, is irreversible.

*Inverted Territory*: complete dissolution creates a new kind of order — scattered islands, each unique and irreplaceable, yet fundamentally alone.

---

### 13 — Organic Terrain

![Organic Terrain](WORKS/PRINT/300p_W5.png)

**Ink on paper — Portrait — 29.7 × 42 × 0.1 cm — 2026 — Original, non-editioned**

Where igneous forms were angular and structured, erosion creates curves — the signatures of water, wind, and biological processes reshaping stone. The central mass shows flowing, organic topography; below it float two isolated fragments as if ejected by the transformation above.

The curves suggest erosion is not destructive but creative — as matter dissolves it generates new shapes, new possibilities. The isolated fragments are not failures but birthings, new entities created through dissolution.

*Organic Terrain*: destruction and creation are not opposites but partners.

---

### 14 — Primal Chaos

![Primal Chaos](WORKS/PRINT/300p_W6.png)

**Ink on paper — Portrait — 29.7 × 42 × 0.1 cm — 2026 — Original, non-editioned**

Chaos as a dynamic force. Lines explode outward from multiple centers, creating a composition of competing energies. Rather than the ordered radiations of *Compressed Time*, *Primal Chaos* shows chaos as creative force — the energy released when geological systems fragment beyond all possibility of reassembly.

The composition vibrates: lines collide, intersect, creating interference patterns. No center, no hierarchy — only the democracy of equal forces pushing outward.

*Primal Chaos*: the moment when dissolution becomes its own kind of unstoppable power.

---

### 15 — Containment Lines

![Containment Lines](WORKS/PRINT/300p_W7.png)

**Ink on paper — Portrait — 29.7 × 42 × 0.1 cm — 2026 — Original, non-editioned**

Geometric straight lines attempt to impose order on eroding, fragmentary topography. Curved organic forms push against the boundaries created by geometric structure. The composition is a struggle between two contradictory logics: the desire to contain and organize versus the reality of dissolution and disorder.

Neither logic wins fully. The lines do not contain the organic forms; erosion does not obliterate structure. They coexist in uneasy tension, each acknowledging the other's power.

*Containment Lines*: the futility of control against geological forces — a tension that can never be resolved, only briefly suspended.

---

### 16 — Fading Lines

![Fading Lines](WORKS/PRINT/300p_W8.png)

**Ink on paper — Landscape — 42 × 29.7 × 0.1 cm — 2026 — Original, non-editioned**

An elegant topographic arc dominates the upper portion — still readable, still graceful despite its isolation. Below, a single fragment floats in vast white emptiness, the arc's lines thinning as it disperses into void.

The topography does not disappear abruptly but dissolves gradually into white void, the isolated fragment suggesting dissolution continues, matter scattering endlessly.

*Fading Lines* is the culmination of the series — the final moment where form returns to emptiness, completing a cycle. Matter remembering its own dissolution: a gentle letting go that contains both loss and the return of new possibilities.

## About the Artist

J3ZZ is an artist from Réunion Island. His work moves at the intersection of deep human history, philosophical inquiry, and the disruptions of contemporary technological society — tracing what persists beneath the surface of a world in accelerating transformation.

Working primarily with ink on paper, he is drawn to processes that cannot be undone. Each mark is irreversible, a record of a singular decision made in time. This irreversibility is not a constraint but a conviction: that the gesture, like history itself, cannot be revised without becoming something else entirely.

His practice embraces the tension between the ancient and the immediate — between geological time and digital speed, between the hand and the algorithm, between the map and the territory it can no longer describe. He sees in modern technologies not a rupture with the human past but a new lens through which older questions return with greater urgency: What endures? What collapses? What do we leave behind?

*Cartography of Collapse* (2026) is his most recent major series, comprising sixteen original ink drawings that chart this territory — geological in form, philosophical in intent, and irreversibly human in execution.
